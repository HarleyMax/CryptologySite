This page will perform operations involving RSA. RSA is an assymetric encryption system. To use RSA, one needs to come up with two very large prime numbers, p and q. Then one must find n = p * q, and the euler's totient of n, which is (p-1) * (q-1). You then choose an integer e such that e is more than 1 but less than the euler's totient of n, and also that the greatest common demoninator of e and the euler's totient of n is 1. Then, you find d such that d is the multiplicate inverse of e mod the euler's totient of n. (e,n) is the public key and (d,n) is the private key.


RSA Encryption

To encrypt with RSA, you must first convert whatever message is being sent to an integer. For this site, the message is converted to an integer by finding the ascii values of each character and combining them into one integer. This integer is then raised to the power of e, the exponent part of the public key, and computed mod n, the modulo part of the public key. For the purposes of this website, e <= 999.

RSA Decryption

To decrypt with RSA, the ciphertext integer is raised to the power of d, the exponent part of the private key, and computed mod n, the modulo part of the private key. The ciphertext must be an integer and for the purposes of this website, d <= 999.
